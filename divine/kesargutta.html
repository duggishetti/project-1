• Keesaragutta Temple is a Hindu sanctuary committed to Lord Siva
  • It is situated on a little hillock. The sanctuary draws a huge number of devotees on Shivaratri.
  • Investigation and Excavations were led by the archeological division in the surroundings of Keesaragutta Temple. Numerous Remnants of Brick structures and Shiva Lingas were found on the Hill north of the sanctuary and a hillock close to the water asset
  • Hanuman came back with 101 lingams for the determination from Varanasi and felt wronged at not having his lingam introduced. Consequently, he tossed all of them over the region. Indeed, even right up 'til the present time a few lingams are discovered scattered everywhere outside the sanctuary.
  • On October 18, 2014, twelve symbols of Jain Tirthankara going back to the fourth fifth century were found close to the sanctuary ventures at a profundity of one foot which demonstrates that Jainism existed together alongside Hinduism at Keesaragutta amid the season of Vishnukundins in 4-fifth century
